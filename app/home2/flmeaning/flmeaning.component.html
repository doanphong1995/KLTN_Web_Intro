Roses

On Valentine's Day or every other occasion of love, roses are an indispensable gift. Because it is the flower that symbolizes the most popular love in the world, is the sweet, passionate flower that love has woven into each petal, stamens and also the subtle aroma subtle. ...
Red roses symbolize a passionate love and romance. Red roses have been around for centuries, and have spread through many cultures - both in the West and in the East. But everywhere, it is always the most loved flower, and symbolizes the sentiment. The same sacred: love.
White roses symbolize innocence, grace and sympathy. White roses not only symbolize a pure, noble love on the other hand, it is also the flower that apologizes to your lover if you make a mistake.
Golden Roses: perhaps this is the flower that gives the recipient a pretty headache because of its rather diverse meaning: Golden Roses not only symbolize a glorious love, but it is also the flower of love. You show interest and progress in relationships, such as "I care about you" or "Remember you". Offering a bouquet of gold roses can be a great start to the day, and a happy return. Sometimes, yellow roses are a manifestation of jealousy, falling love, and betrayal. Besides, the yellow rose is also the ideal flower for mother and grandmother, because it also has the meaning of gratitude and respect.
Pink roses: The light pink roses of roses imply the beginning of a dreamlike, romantic and tender love. Besides, it also shows a full happiness with the message: "Believe me!"
Orange roses: Express enthusiasm and burning passion. The brilliant orange color expresses a passionate love but is mixed with jealousy. Or another meaning, it is the bridge between friendship and love (by the mix of gold and red) and implies that "I am proud of you."
Purple roses: In addition to the inherent elegance, purple roses show passion, loyalty, and intense love at first sight. Green roses symbolize an eternal love.
